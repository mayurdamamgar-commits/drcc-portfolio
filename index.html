1. INTRODUCTION

This course project focuses on the structural design of load-carrying members for a residential (living) building. The aim is to understand the behavior, design principles, and code provisions involved in designing reinforced concrete members subjected to dead loads, live loads, and environmental effects.

All designs are carried out in accordance with Indian Standard Codes, ensuring safety, serviceability, and durability.

ELEMENT DESIGN

Following the analysis, the reinforcement detailing is carried out. Emphasis is placed on ductilty and confinement in beam-column joints.

RCC Beam

Flexural reinforcement designed. Shear reinforcement (stirrups) provided. Deflection control checks applied.

RCC Column

Designed using interaction diagrams. Minimum and maximum reinforcement as per IS 456. Lateral ties provided.

RCC Slab

One-way / Two-way slab. Effective depth calculated based on span. Checks for shear and deflection carried out.

Foundation

Safe bearing capacity considered. Bending, shear, and punching shear checks performed.

SITE VISIT REPORT

Flat Slab Structural System with Drop Panels

1. INTRODUCTION

A detailed site inspection was carried out at a multi-storey RCC building constructed using a Flat Slab Structural System with Column Drop Panels. The visit aimed to understand reinforcement layout, slab‚Äìcolumn junction detailing, shuttering practices, load transfer mechanism, and construction sequencing. Flat slabs are increasingly used in India due to architectural flexibility, faster construction, and better accommodation of services.

2. PURPOSE OF VISIT

  • ‚ñ∏ Study reinforcement arrangement in flat slabs
  • ‚ñ∏ Understand drop panel detailing & punching shear
  • ‚ñ∏ Observe formwork and shuttering systems
  • ‚ñ∏ Review construction practices & safety

3. PROJECT DETAILS

System Flat slab + Drop panels
Floor Slab without beams
Shuttering Waterproof plywood
Steel HYSD Fe-500
Slab Thk 150‚Äì180 mm
Drop Thk 225‚Äì300 mm

4. FLAT SLAB SYSTEM

A structural arrangement where the slab rests directly on columns without beams. Loads transfer directly from slab ‚Üí column ‚Üí foundation.

ADVANTAGES
  • Increased clear floor height
  • Easy routing of MEP services
  • Faster construction
LIMITATIONS
  • Punching shear risk
  • Requires precise detailing

5. DROP PANELS

Importance: Increases effective depth, reduces shear stress, and improves negative moment capacity at supports.
IS 456 Provisions: Drop width ‚â• 1/6 span | Drop thickness ‚â• 1.25 √ó slab thickness
